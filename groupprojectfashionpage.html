Eco-Friendly Fashion
What is Eco-Friendly Fashion?
Eco-friendly fashion is the concept of sustainable fashion, and being a conscious consumer when it comes to clothing choices. Currently, it is a social movement that aims to lower the environmental impacts that the clothing industry creates, due to excessive waste, carbon emissions, and overall pollution.

A major aspect of eco-friendly clothing is avoiding fast fashion- items that are based on the micro-trends of the internet, as the fashion cycle is so quick in recent times, that items come and go out of trend within months, meaning that lower-quality items are being mass produced from fast fashion companies in order to meet the trend-based demand, creating excessive, and unnecessary waste.
Solutions
To create a more eco-friendly wardrobe, you can start small, and work your way up, as drastic changes are unrealistic for the majority of people. To begin with, if the actual items being shopped for are difficult to change, you can try deliveries that are more carbon-neutral and use eco-friendly packaging with minimal waste. Along with this, you can try to avoid one-time use packaging, and try sharing shipping items to reduce the amount of packaging used.

In terms of the clothing, many changes can be made. To start small, you can implement practices of thrifting and repurposing clothes by having them tailored, or alter them yourself. Along with this, more clothing rental services can be implemented to reduce the number of wasted or forgotten clothing items sitting in people’s closets, and could potentially be cheaper than simply buying an item, especially with more formal or high-end attire. In terms of new purchases, consumers should be more mindful to buy items they genuinely like and will wear multiple times, rather than buying low-quality, fast-fashion, trend-cycle based items, to ensure materials are not being wasted. Along with this, investing in items from eco-friendly fashion companies is key, as mass interest will drive away profits from cheaper brands, allowing eco-friendly fashion to become more common and financially accessible overtime, whilst helping the environment. The eco-friendly fashion space can be approached in a plethora of ways, but what truly matters is that people simply make a change, whether big or small, because every change counts, and every action matters.

Products & Companies
Outerknown

This brand creates timeless designs that can outlast any trend, with simple, yet elegant patterns, along with reliable quality and easy to style versatility. Their pieces use 95% recycled fibers, and up to 100% in their trunks. Along with this, they make constant efforts to prioritize fair labor- moving up to 67% of the supply chain from their country of origin to ensure all human rights are respected and protected in their clothing creation. The prices are on the higher end compared to a baseline, common brand, however they make up for it to account for protecting human rights, and prioritizing eco-friendly production. Their timeless items are an investment worth making in the long term, for you, and the planet.

image5

Everlane

Everlane is a brand that sells modern staples, and classic items that are versatile and reliable as daily wear items, making their collection worthwhile to invest in for the long term. In terms of their fabrics, 98% of the polyester and nylon is recycled, and 82% of their cotton is certified organic, recycled, regenerative, or fully traceable. Along with the clothes themselves, their packaging of protective bags are made of 100% eco-friendly materials, and their manufacturing staff earn above minimum wage in their respective countries, and have active trade unions. Everlane is an all rounder in that they are sustainable and eco-friendly with the clothing itself, but also with its packaging, and production. The best part is that they do all of that, while being modern and timeless, perfect for anyone’s collection.

image6

Pact
This brand sells an affordable, timeless collection of everyday clothing basics and activewear, and prioritizes the wellbeing of the planet, and those who live on it. Currently, they use organic cotton, sustainable crops that use 81% less water and 62% less energy for production, and prioritize the fair treatment of their staff. They are a brand always looking to improve, with programs like their “Give Back Box,” and constant improvements on their sustainability goals. On top of that, they are very accessible to the common person, as their items are affordable for many people, whilst being reliable, and effortless.

Kotn is another brand that combines timeless pieces with affordable prices to make eco-friendly fashion as accessible as possible. They source their raw cotton from smaller farmers in Egypt, with traceable materials and non-toxic dyes, as well as biodegradable fibers that can biodegrade back into the earth. Kotn products are built to last, and are classic designs that will last for many years to come, while being relatively accessible at their price point.
